# Project: You, per p5.js 🎨

## What this is
This project is your **personal logo** — a small p5.js sketch that shows the class who you
are. Using code, you'll put your name, a few pictures of things you care about, and a
personal motto on the screen. There's no single "right answer" here — the goal is to make
something that looks like **you**.

Along the way you'll get comfortable with two tools you'll use throughout your p5.js
career: the **`text()`** command and the **sketch tools** that help you draw.

## What to make (three parts)

### 1. Your name
Put your name — the name you want to be called — on the canvas using the `text()` command.
- Reference for `text()`: https://p5js.org/reference/#/p5/text
- Example:
  ```js
  textSize(48);
  fill("black");
  text("Your Name", 100, 80);
  ```
  The two numbers are the **x and y position** — where the text goes on the canvas.

> 💡 **Teaser:** `text()` isn't only for letters — emoji work too, like
> `text("🎸", 200, 200)`. (Notice the 🎨 in the tools panel? Same trick.) What else could
> you fit inside `text()`?

### 2. Three to five pictures that show us who you are
Make **3–5 icons or small pictures** that show your passions, hobbies, and interests —
things that tell us what you care about. Some ideas: a soccer ball, a music note, a
mountain, a game controller, a pet, a favorite food.
- Use the **sketch tools** to draw at least a few of these. That's exactly what they're
  for, and they make it much easier.
- If a word says it better than a picture, you may use `text()` for **some** of them — but
  you must include **some drawings that you made yourself**, too.

### 3. Your motto
Add a short **personal motto or mantra** — a few words that matter to you. Something you
want to carry with you and remind yourself of as you go.
- There is no wrong motto. It can be serious or it can be fun.
- It's okay if your motto changes later — most do. This one captures **where you are right
  now**, and that's the whole point.

## How to use the sketch tools
1. Run the sketch and **open your browser console** (instructions are in the starter code).
2. **Click** on the canvas to place points for a shape.
3. The matching p5.js **code appears in the console** — copy it and paste it into your
   `draw()` function.
4. Press **`h`** to show or hide the helper panel. Use the color pickers to choose colors.
5. When you are finished, **turn the tools off** before you submit (comment out
   `enableSketchTools` — the panel reminds you how).

## Tips for success
- Start simple. Get your name on the screen first, then add one picture at a time.
- Pick colors you like — try typing a fancy color name, not just "red." 😉
- Save your work often.

- [ ] My **motto** is on the canvas.

Have fun with this one — it's the assignment where looking like *you* is the assignment.
